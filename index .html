Makeblock Ultimate 2.0 Kit

The Makeblock Ultimate 2.0 Kit is a comprehensive robot kit for beginners to explore the robotic world or experts to build complex robots. It contains everything you need to construct your robot dreams: metal geared motors, Arduino-compatible controller, programmable RGB LED strip, different kinds of sensors, robotic gripper and other mechanical parts. It is also great for learning Arduino and Scratch programming. You can control your robot with your smart device through a Bluetooth connection.

Features

  • Great for learning robotics, electronics and Arduino programming
  • Aluminum extrusion structure
  • Easy wiring, no soldering required
  • Bluetooth connection lets you control your robot with your smartphone or tablet

Programming and Control

Smart device app Control (free of coding): Install the Makeblock app to control your creations with your Android or iOS device.

Scratch programming (Graphical programming tool suitable for your kids): Makeblock platform supports graphical programming language Scratch, you can simply drag and joint the blocks to run your creations without difficult coding.

Arduino programming

Arduino is an open-source electronics prototyping platform based on flexible, easy to use hardware and software. The Arduino software consists of a development environment (IDE) and the core libraries. The IDE is written in Java and based on the Processing development environment. The core libraries are written in C and C++ and compiled using avr-gcc and AVR Libc.

Arduino Uno

The Arduino Uno is a widely used open-source microcontroller board designed for beginners and experienced users alike, serving as a foundational platform for electronics and coding projects. It is based on the ATmega328P microcontroller and is known for its robustness, extensive documentation, and large community support.

Key Features and Specifications

The Arduino Uno is equipped with 14 digital input/output pins, six of which can be used for Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) outputs, and 6 analog input pins. It operates at 5V and is recommended to be powered with an input voltage between 7-12V, though it can handle a limit of 6-20V. The board features a 16 MHz ceramic resonator, a USB connection for programming and power, a power jack for external power sources, an ICSP header, and a reset button.

  • Microcontroller: ATmega328P
  • Operating Voltage: 5V
  • Input Voltage (Recommended): 7-12V
  • Digital I/O Pins: 14 (6 with PWM output)
  • Analog Input Pins: 6
  • DC Current per I/O Pin: 20 mA
  • Flash Memory: 32 KB (0.5 KB used by bootloader)
  • SRAM: 2 KB
  • EEPROM: 1 KB
  • Clock Speed: 16 MHz

Power and Connectivity

The Arduino Uno can be powered via its USB connection or an external power supply, with the board automatically selecting the source. External power can be supplied through a DC barrel jack or by connecting a battery to the VIN and GND pins. The board communicates with a computer via a USB Type-B cable using the ATmega16U2 microcontroller, which acts as a USB-to-serial converter. It also supports serial communication (UART TTL) on digital pins 0 (RX) and 1 (TX), I2C on pins A4 (SDA) and A5 (SCL), and SPI on pins 10 (SS), 11 (MOSI), 12 (MISO), and 13 (SCK).

Programming and Development

Programming the Arduino Uno is done using the Arduino IDE (Integrated Development Environment), which supports a C++-like programming language. The ATmega328P microcontroller comes with a pre-programmed bootloader, allowing new code to be uploaded without an external hardware programmer. The Uno is also an open-source hardware design, meaning its schematics and production files are publicly available, allowing for modification and custom builds.

Advantages and Limitations

The Arduino Uno's popularity stems from its user-friendly interface, extensive community support, and compatibility with numerous add-on boards called "shields." Its affordability and open-source nature also contribute to its widespread adoption. However, its limitations include relatively small memory capacity (32 KB flash, 2 KB SRAM, 1 KB EEPROM) and a moderate clock speed of 16 MHz, which may not be suitable for highly complex or data-intensive projects. It also lacks integrated wireless communication features like Wi-Fi or Bluetooth.

mBot - Entry Level Robot Kit

The mBot is an entry-level educational robot kit designed to make learning about robotics and programming fun and accessible for beginners. It is developed by Makeblock and is aimed at children to help them develop skills in Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM).

Core Features and Educational Value

The mBot is designed for easy assembly, often requiring only a screwdriver and taking about 10-15 minutes to put together. It utilizes color-coded RJ25 ports for simplified wiring. The robot comes with pre-programmed control modes, such as obstacle avoidance, line following, and manual control, allowing for immediate interaction and play.

  • Ease of Assembly: Designed for quick and simple construction without soldering.
  • Programming: Supports graphical, block-based programming through the mBlock software (based on Scratch 3.0) and can also be programmed using the Arduino IDE for more advanced users.
  • Sensors and Modules: Equipped with various sensors like light, ultrasonic, and line-following sensors, as well as programmable modules like RGB LEDs and a buzzer.
  • Expandability: Compatible with Makeblock's ecosystem of electronic and mechanical parts, as well as LEGO bricks, allowing for extensive customization and expansion through add-on packs.

Software and Programming

The mBot can be controlled and programmed using several software options:

  • mBlock (App/PC): A powerful, block-based programming platform derived from Scratch 3.0, which also supports a transition to text-based programming like Arduino C and Python.
  • Makeblock App: A standard mobile application for controlling and programming Makeblock robots.
  • mBlock Blockly App: Game-based programming software designed for STEAM education.

Technical Specifications

The mBot is built around an ATmega328 microcontroller. It typically runs on a 3.7V lithium battery or four AA batteries. Its dimensions are approximately 190 × 130 × 130 mm when assembled, and it weighs around 500g. Connectivity options often include Bluetooth or a 2.4G wireless connection, depending on the specific version.

ASHS History

Apayao Science High School Today

Located on top of a mountain viewing the beauty of nature stands the Apayao Science High School, that was created under RA 8896 authored by then Congressman of the Lone District of Apayao, Hon. ELIAS K. BULUT. This converted the High School Department of the Apayao Stage College to Apayao Science High School located at San Isidro Sur, Luna, Apayao on August 22, 2000.

Tracing its origin, the high school department of the Apayao State Colledge was the former Luna Academy, a private school that became an annex of the Kaling-Apayao Schools of Arts and Trades located at Malama, Conner, Apayao on June 1, 1993. As an annex the high school department maintained the curriculum of the private school and the former school of the Luna Academy, Betty L. Racacho was assigned as the Officer In-Charge for both high school and college department for four school years. From 1997-2002 Betty L. Racacho opted to lead and manage the secondary department only. Some teachers from the main campus namely Marieta Lacuesta, Marcolino Manera, Rosemarie Delloro, Evelyn Mamauag, Marieta Bayaua, Cecilio Culili, Bienvenido Miranda, Florida Bono, were detailed as well as teacher of the Luna Academy, Rosedella A. Guieb, Rizalene R. Licuben, Bejamin N. Lactam, Elmer S. Pelobello, Sonia A. Belarde, Everlyn P. Alvarez, Luningning B. Aribbay, were also accommodated with status as emergency teachers, while Edgar Valdez, Herminio Ulep were added in the teaching staff being paid by the Region.

From KASAT, this school was again converted to Apayao Institute of Science and Technology – Luna Campus and finally in 1998 this became the Apayao State College – Luna Campus. With the conversion of the name of the school, the high school department of the annex was not included in the budget because according to CHED policy there must be only one training department. The high school department survived because of the unending support of then honorable Congressman Elias C. Bulut, Jr., Governor Elias K. Bulut, LGU family headed by the late Mayor Betty C. Verzola, civic-minded citizens, school canteen and the generous officers and members of the Parents-Teachers Association. This is believed to be one of the reasons that the high school department of the Apayao State College was converted to Apayao Science High School.

Former Luna Academy

After the signing of the RA 8896 in 2000, Hon. Elias K. Bulut, Sr. opened the Apayao Science High School in the next school year 2001-2002. On the first year of operation ASHS operated without any budget in the GAA. Betty L. Racacho was assigned as Teacher In-Charge, with her are the following teachers who taught in the first year of operation of the said school, Ruth A. Tumaneng, Reymarie M. Caban, Edgar S. Valdez, Luningning B. Aribbay, Romy A. Rigates, Antonio Manuel, Sonia A. Belarde, Crispina Pelobello, Cristeta Arimas, Leah Acidera, Dely Aguite, Sylvia F. Baria, Marcel Ubay and Imelda Bautista. Just the same the school survived with our benevolent supporters, On the 2nd year of operation, Roasalia D. Guadañia, Ed.D., the chief of the Secondary Education of the DepED CAR came to Apayao Science High School as the Acting director of the said school with new sets of teachers namely, Betty L. Racacho, Acdemic Chief – Designate, Mercuria T. Menor, Evelyn M. Tabangcura, Clarissa D. Vidad, Noble Marissa C. Domingo, Mary Jane T. Trumpo, Salome F. Baria, Sheryl E. Parugrug, Gladys A. Aguibitin, Marcel Al T. Trumpo, Maribel Mitra, Don John N. Fronda, Myra Colis, Julius Paleracion, Vincent Labayog, Vilfred C. Duldulao and Ardenliz R. Balicat. This school has an annual budget of Fifty Thousand pesos (PhP 50,000.00). With the stay of Madam Guadana, she worked on the papers of the school to operate as indicated in the RA 8896. She conducted meetings with concerned offices and of course the office of the congressman. Ath the end of the school year 2002-2003 particularly March, 2003 Madam Gaudana retired, and from the time she retired the papers to be worked on specially the Implementing Rules and Regulations for the science high stopped. The school was left to Region P. Tomas handling 3 secondary schools in Luna and in the school 2004-2005 the school was manned by Mercuria T. Menor until June 30, 2011.

On January 5, 2007, the 1-storey building with three rooms was burned which includes the newly purchased laboratory apparatuses, books, set of encyclopedias solicited from Hawaii by the officers of the PTA, printers, sports equipment and personal reference of teachers, thus classes were conducted at the San Isidro Gymnasium for two weeks. Since the conduct of classes inside the Gymnasium is very inappropriate for Science classes, a parent who saw the difficulties in teaching inside the gymnasium who happens to be an employee of the Department of Agriculture (BPI) offered their stock rooms and seed production rooms as temporary classrooms. ASHS was thankful of that Good Samaritan employee/parent who in the person of Angie Oliviano. They were accommodated there until the end of the school year.

The following School Year (SY 2007-2008), the school was lucky to have a parent and at the same time a HRPTA President of Class Galilei in the of Mrs. Digna Villanueva who was one of the high-ranking officers of the BPI-Research outreach then (now Department of Agriculture). Through her, Mrs. Mercuria T. Menor the Teacher In-Charge was able to approach Cesar D. Rodriguez, the Regional Director of the Department of Agriculture, Regional Field Unit, Cordillera Administrative Region on those times and able to request the legality of the site given to Apayao Science High School and thus, the Deed of Grant of Usufructuary Rights was handed over.

And it was formally handed on January 13, 2008 at Soriano’s Notary Public Office, Abulug, Cagayan.

On July 4, 2011, Betty L. Racacho was designated as the Teacher In-Charge. It is at this time that she referred to the Hon. Congresman the status of the IRR that drafted last school year 2002-2003. With the approval of the IRR, this would very much the operation of the school.

Apayao Science High School Today

In 2020, the Schools Division of Apayao through SDS Benedicta B. Gamatero assigned Mr. Danilo L. Cudiamat to head the school. This was the second year of the COVID 19 pandemic. In response, he and the whole teaching staff established online platforms such as the Student Information and Accounting System (SIAS)- LMS together with available online applications in order to ensure learning continuity amidst the challenges posed by the pandemic. In 2021, the school crafted Robotics Curriculum Guides from Grade 7-10 which was implemented first in the SY 2022-2023. This gained the support of the Provincial Government of Apayao and House of Representative which led to the provision of financial assistance to the school.

Currently, the school administration is working on the full implementation of the IRR in order to ensure that the needed materials are provided and appropriate funds for the activities of a Science High School are provided to the full advantage of the learners.
